## 2026-06-12 — Core layout decisions (user picks via previews)

- **D1 — Unified roam-nav bar:** Trail and Date Stepper share ONE slim row, mounted in its own row directly under `.rm-topbar` and above `.roam-article`. Trail left-aligned, Stepper right-aligned. On a non-DNP page the right (Stepper) zone is empty/hidden; the Trail zone persists. Strongly serves SM-C1 ("one slim row") + north-star.
- **D2 — Overflow = horizontal scroll:** when crumbs exceed width the bar scrolls horizontally (wheel/trackpad/drag); NO dropdown. ⚠️ CONFLICTS with PRD FR-4 + Assumption C (which specified ~5 inline crumbs + dropdown). User chose scroll deliberately when offered both. UX spine wins → scroll. ACTION: offer to sync PRD at finalize.
- **D3 — Stepper arrows always dated:** controls render the adjacent target dates inline, e.g. `‹ Jun 11   June 12th, 2026   Jun 13 ›` (in the unified compact bar: `‹ Jun 11 · Jun 13 ›`, with the full current date being the page H1 below). No hover-only reveal.

## 2026-06-12 — Reasonable defaults (assumptions, to confirm at finalize)

- **A1 — Bar persistence/visibility:** the roam-nav bar is always present once a Trail Root exists (root defaults to today's DNP per PRD FR-6), i.e. effectively always visible. [ASSUMPTION]
- **A2 — Sticky:** the bar is sticky directly under the top bar; it stays visible while the article scrolls (supports "one glance to orient"). [ASSUMPTION]
- **A3 — Root icon:** the DNP Trail Root carries a small 📅 affordance to distinguish it from page crumbs; separator between crumbs is `›`. [ASSUMPTION]
- **A4 — No shift-to-sidebar in v1:** clicking a crumb/arrow navigates the main window only (PRD: sidebar out of v1). Shift+click→sidebar is a v2 note. [ASSUMPTION]
- **A5 — Truncation of long titles:** individual crumb labels middle-ellipsis at a max width even within the scrolling bar, so no single title dominates. [ASSUMPTION]
- **A6 — A11y floor (no keyboard nav v1):** arrows/crumbs are real buttons/links with aria-labels (e.g. "Previous day, June 11th"), visible focus ring, hit target ≥ 24px, color via theme tokens for contrast in light/dark. [ASSUMPTION]
